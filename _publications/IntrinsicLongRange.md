## Abstract
A stable and long-range antiferromagnetic (AFM) coupling without charge carrier mediators has been searched 
for a long time, but the existence of this kind of coupling is still lacking. Based on first principle 
calculations, we systematically study carrier free long-range AFM coupling in four transition metal 
chalcopyrite systems: $$\text{ABTe}_2$$ ($$\text{A}=\text{Cu}$$ or $$\text{Ag}$$, $$\text{B}=\text{Ga}$$ or 
$$\text{In}$$) in the dilute doping case. The AFM coupling is mainly 
due to the $$p-d$$ coupling and electron redistribution along the interacting chains. The relatively small energy 
difference between $$p$$ and $$d$$ orbitals, as well as between dopants and atoms in the middle of the chain can 
enhance the stability of long- range AFM configurations. A multi-band Hubbard model is proposed to provide 
fundamental understanding of long-range AFM coupling.
